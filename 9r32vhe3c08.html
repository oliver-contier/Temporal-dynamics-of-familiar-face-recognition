Two previous fMRI studies have focused on the changes in familiarity effects over a longer time scale (Gobbini and Haxby, 2006; Kosaka, Omori, Iidaka, Murata, Shimoyama, Okada, Sadato, Yonekura, and Wada, 2003). Kosaka et al. (2003) have investigated how the response to unfamiliar faces habituates over their experiment, which comprised of several functional runs. They found habituation effects in the right amygdala and left fusiform gyrus. The bilateral posterior cingulate gyrus however showed an increase of activity as the experiment proceeded.
